Location

Immersed in nature. Connected to everything.

Las Lomas sits in the Chirripó area of Pérez Zeledón, one of Costa Rica's most biodiverse regions. The property lies within the COBAS (Corredor Biológico Alexander Skutch), a protected bird corridor of international significance, and directly adjacent to Parque Nacional La Amistad, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Despite this pristine natural setting, the city of San Isidro de El General is just 15 minutes away; full-service hospitals, international schools, supermarkets, banks, and commercial centers. The best of both worlds.

Frequently Asked Questions

What developers ask us.

What is Las Lomas and where is it located?

Las Lomas is a 164-hectare finca (approximately 405 acres) located in Santa Elena, Pérez Zeledón, Costa Rica. The property sits at 1,000 meters above sea level in the southern highlands, just 15 minutes from San Isidro de El General and at the foot of Chirripó, the country's highest peak.

What is SETENA D1 clearance and why does it matter?

SETENA is Costa Rica's environmental review body. A D1 classification means the property has already passed the most rigorous environmental viability assessment. This is typically the longest and most expensive regulatory hurdle in Costa Rican land development. With D1 clearance secured, a developer can proceed directly to planning and construction.

How many lots can be developed on the property?

The land's topography and existing SETENA D1 environmental clearance support the segregation of approximately 150 individual residential lots, making it ideal for a phased community development.

What is RISE and how is it related to Las Lomas?

RISE is a family-focused intentional community of approximately 400 acres located directly adjacent to Las Lomas. It is home to full-time families, a Waldorf-inspired school, a private airstrip, Kinkára luxury retreat center, and a fiber-optic co-working hub. Las Lomas borders RISE, giving a future developer immediate access to this established infrastructure and buyer demand.

Does the property have water and road access?

Yes. Las Lomas has 20 kilometers of river running through the property, approved water well concessions already in place, and 20 kilometers of public road frontage along its boundary. These are among the most difficult and costly infrastructure elements to secure in Costa Rica.

What is the asking price for Las Lomas?

The property is offered at $6,000,000 USD. Given the 164-hectare size, existing SETENA D1 clearance, water concessions, road frontage, and adjacency to the RISE community, this represents a unique opportunity for developers seeking pre-approved land in Costa Rica's most desirable highland region.

Why is this called "the last land"?

The geography of this valley means no additional land of this scale can be developed adjacent to RISE. Las Lomas is the only remaining property with the size, elevation, and proximity to benefit from the existing community infrastructure. Once acquired, this position cannot be replicated.
