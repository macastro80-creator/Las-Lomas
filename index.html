What is Las Lomas

A 164-hectare finca in Santa Elena, Pérez Zeledón, sitting at 1,000 meters above sea level. It holds SETENA D1 environmental clearance, meaning it is already approved for development. The land offers panoramic mountain views, natural water sources, and road access. It is offered at $6M.

What is RISE, and why it matters

RISE is the 400-acre community directly adjacent to Las Lomas. It is home to a Waldorf-inspired school, a private airstrip with hangars, Kinkára luxury retreat center, a fiber-optic co-working hub, and an organic farm. The residents are entrepreneurs, remote founders, and families with resources who chose this valley intentionally. RISE is thriving, and Las Lomas sits right next to all of it.

Why the adjacency changes everything

A developer acquiring Las Lomas doesn't need to build demand or infrastructure from zero. The school, the airstrip, the retreat center, the co-working space: it's all already there, next door. The people who would buy in a Las Lomas development already live at RISE or are on the waitlist to get in. The buyer profile is proven. The demand is real.

The last available land

The geography of this valley means there is no more developable land adjacent to RISE. Las Lomas is the final piece. Once it's acquired, this position cannot be replicated. There is nothing else like it.
